About

Engineering team from Kyiv

ContiMech is a small engineering team working with robotics, automation, embedded systems, mechanics, electronics, firmware, software, testing, and technical documentation.

Background

Academic and industrial base

The team combines university engineering background with work in product companies, service companies, and industrial projects. This gives us a practical view of both technical decisions and delivery constraints.

Our experience covers automotive-oriented tooling, industrial engineering, production process modeling, robotic prototypes, embedded systems, and engineering documentation.

Working principle

One engineering process

We try to keep mechanics, hardware, firmware, software, QA, and documentation connected from the beginning. This reduces gaps between design, implementation, testing, and handover.

Capabilities

What the team can cover

Mechanics

Parts and housings

Enclosures, brackets, fixtures, mounting concepts, cable routing, assembly constraints, and CAD support.

Hardware

Boards and interfaces

PCB development support, connector selection, power domains, signal interfaces, bring-up, measurements, and hardware documentation.

Firmware

Low-level software

MCU firmware, drivers, communication protocols, diagnostics, configuration logic, and test utilities.

Software

Tools and integration

Desktop utilities, integration software, ROS 2 nodes, model-based design, simulation support, and control logic.

QA

Manual verification

Test cases, smoke checks, regression checks, acceptance testing, evidence collection, and structured reports.

Documentation

Engineering artifacts

Requirements, architecture notes, interface descriptions, diagrams, test plans, risk notes, and handover packages.

Domains

Where the experience is strongest

  • Robotics and robotic prototypes.
  • Industrial automation and automation of technical processes.
  • Automotive-oriented systems, ECU-related tooling, and validation support.
  • Process modeling for industrial and production environments.
  • Embedded hardware, firmware, control systems, and system integration.
Methods

Engineering methods

  • Model-based approach for simulation, control logic, and process analysis.
  • AI-assisted code generation for drafts, boilerplate, and routine tasks.
  • Handwritten code for critical firmware, drivers, interfaces, and algorithms.
  • Documentation-first work for maintainability and handover.
