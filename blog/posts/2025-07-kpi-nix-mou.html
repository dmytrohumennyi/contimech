N-iX × KPI: a memorandum that actually works

2025-07-01 • Kyiv · Igor Sikorsky KPI
Partnership Education R&D Prototypes

In brief

Growth makes sense where education, science, and business meet in concrete engineering problems. That is exactly what the N-iX × Igor Sikorsky KPI memorandum is about.

We did not set up this cooperation for formality. The intent was to launch joint R&D topics, build prototypes, and strengthen engineer preparation before the first job offer even appears.

Personal context

I was one of the initiators of this memorandum. For me, N-iX and KPI are two organizations that shape my professional focus: applied engineering in business and strong scientific schools within the university.

That is why I am genuinely glad not only that the cooperation started, but that it moved into regular work very quickly.

What we are doing together

  • Joint prototypes at the intersection of business and science, with both practical value and scientific novelty.
  • Joint participation in international technical events to show actual results, not just ideas.
  • Educational initiatives: updating curricula, reviewing student projects, and aligning topics with real engineering needs.

Why this is not just decorative cooperation

A few months after signing, it was already obvious that this is not a “headline-only” agreement. We coordinate weekly at KPI: topics, progress, expectations, and artifacts.

One major direction now is a new academic program that combines industry practice and university fundamentals: system design, prototyping, validation, documentation, and teamwork.

About KPI

KPI is a university with strong technical schools and a deep scientific tradition. It graduates around 4,000 engineers every year, approximately one third of whom move into IT-related careers.

That is the right scale and environment for building long-term R&D relationships and preparing engineers for real industry challenges.

Conclusion

Partnerships like this are about building an ecosystem: companies do not just hire graduates, they invest, support, and co-create with the university.

If you work in R&D or education and want to build a university ↔ industry bridge with concrete artifacts, feel free to contact me.
