KNUBA master's pre-defense (KBKI), December 2025: outcomes and observations

2025-12-18 • Kyiv · KNUBA
KNUBA Education Quality State Examination Board

In brief

From 15 to 18 December 2025, I participated in the master's pre-defense at the KBKI department of KNUBA as Deputy Chair of the State Examination Board. Roughly 85% of students were recommended for defense; the rest were not recommended after a collegial review.

Summary

  • Format: master's pre-defense at the KBKI department of KNUBA.
  • Role: Deputy Chair of the State Examination Board.
  • Outcome: around 85% of works were recommended for defense; the rest were not recommended after discussion by the board.

Observations

  • In some cases, submitted materials did not match the approved topic and work plan previously agreed with the supervisor.
  • There were also examples of inappropriate communication from students. This is incompatible with academic standards and with the process of collegial review.
  • In such situations, a decision of non-admission protects the quality of the program, the reputation of the diploma, and the real expectations of employers.

Conclusion

I am grateful to my colleagues for a balanced and unanimous decision. For me, this is an example of how procedure and quality principles work in practice — without ignoring real risks.

Note: this post contains no personal student data; the observations are generalized.
