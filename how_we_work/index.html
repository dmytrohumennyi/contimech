Two-Dimensional Workflow: Phases × Streams

This page explains our process for both business and technical audiences. The diagram below shows how three engineering streams progress through the phases. Circles mark checkpoints; they map to concrete artifacts and exit criteria. NDA-safe and client-agnostic by design.

For business stakeholders
  • Predictable scope & milestones: Phase 0 locks goals, constraints, risks, success criteria.
  • Fast feasibility (Phase 1): A-sample demonstrates the core principle and de-risks the path.
  • Design transparency (Phase 2): Architecture and interfaces documented, reviewed, versioned.
  • Evidence-driven delivery (Phase 3–4): Implementation with measurable KPIs, tests, and reports.
  • Certification readiness (Phase 5): Full traceability chain packaged for audits and market entry.
  • Operations: Knowledge transfer, support playbooks, and maintenance windows.
For engineers
  • System Engineering: Requirements (IDs, baselines), change control, hazards & mitigations.
  • Hardware & Mechanics: Prototypes, thermal/load analysis, DFM/DFA, BOM & ECNs.
  • Software: Interfaces/contracts, modular architecture, diagnostics/logging, CI/CD.
  • Verification: Unit/integration/system, HIL where relevant, E2E regression and coverage.
  • Artifacts: Specs, models, test plans & reports, manufacturing docs, evidence folders.
  • Gates: Entry/exit criteria per phase; objective readiness checks before moving forward.
Reading the matrix

Each circle is a stream×phase checkpoint (e.g., S2 — System Engineering in Architecture phase). Clicking a node can lead to deeper documentation in the future.

System Engineering (S✱)

  • S0: Objectives, constraints, risks, success metrics.
  • S1: Feasibility criteria, assumptions, updated risk log.
  • S2: Architecture views, traceability matrix, verification plan.
  • S3: Requirements stabilization, change requests, review minutes.
  • S4: Reliability & compliance evidence, updated hazard analysis.
  • S5: Certification package index and audit mapping.

Hardware & Mechanics (H✱)

  • H0: High-level concept, preliminary BoM outlook.
  • H1: Prototype bring-up notes, bench results.
  • H2: Mechanical drawings, thermal/load simulations.
  • H3: Prototype revisions, test fixtures, measurement logs.
  • H4: DFM/DFA changes, extended tests, pre-compliance checks.
  • H5: Manufacturing docs, test procedures, traceability.

Software (W✱)

  • W0: Interface assumptions, constraints & risks.
  • W1: Prototype code spikes, feasibility metrics.
  • W2: Architecture diagrams or models; contracts & timing.
  • W3: Implementation, diagnostics, CI, unit/integration tests.
  • W4: System tests, performance, long-run stability, regression gates.
  • W5: Evidence bundle: coverage, results, release notes, support plan.
